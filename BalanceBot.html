Self-Balancing Bot Design

by Benjamin Miranda

A self balancing robot. NOT DONE YET

In this project I am creating a schematic and PCB that will be able to power and control a self balancing robot. I dont know if I will ever create a full working robot, as that will take some mechanical engineering and CAD design skills that I am not the best at, and this project is to develop my electrical enginnering design skills. I am going to pretend that a mechanical design is made and just needs electronics. In order make a working robot certain electronic components have to be picked to acheive the design plan. Here are some of the essentials for the design:

  • Microcontroller
  • Power
  • Motors and Motor Controllers
  • Accelerometer Sensor

Microcontroller

In order to select a Microcontroller for the project, I want something that was somewhat easy to use and program. I wanted to move beyond Arduinos so I choose its better counterpart, and ESP32. Specificaly a ESP32-S3-WROOM. For the schematic/PCB, I followed the layout of commercial boards. This involved the UART to Serial Chip integration and the USB connection port.

Power

Power is the most important part of the project. I plan on a 6S battery (~22.2V) being used. This voltage will be used to power the motors. Next is make use of buck convertors to drop the voltage to 3.3V and 5v that can be used by the Microcontroller and other peripherals/sensors. I chose switching voltage regulators over linear regulators as this is a mobile application and effiencey is important to sustain battery life. I choose a chip that would be able to output 3A which is enough to power the various elctronics
